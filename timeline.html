CHANEL Fragrance: Dates of Importance

Review the events on the Timeline and when ready click the 'Test Me' button, to test your knowledge!

19-8-1883

Gabrielle Chanel was born.

1883

After her mother passed away, Gabrielle Chanel was sent to Aubazine Orphanage.

1901

She worked at Moulins in Paris as a singer.

1909

Unofficial to Arthur 'Boy' Capel.

1912

She opened her first boutique in Deauville.

1915

She opened her Haute Couture Boutique in Biarritz.

1918

Mademoiselle opened her couture house at 31 Rue Cambon in Paris.

22-12-1919

Arthur Boy Capel dies.

1921

Gabrielle Chanel met Grand Duke Dimitri. She also unveils her first fragrance Chanel No. 5.

1923

She met the Duke of Westminster and was with him for 7 years influencing her to create the Tweed jacket.

1926

Coco Chanel created the 'Little Black Dress' and started doing costume jewellery.

1932

Gabrielle Chanel created her first High Jewellery collection "Bijoux De Diamants" Celebrating the brilliance and purity of diamonds.

1954

At the age of 71, Gabrielle Chanel stages the grand re-opening of her couture house and first creates her iconic Tweed Jacket.

1955

Gabrielle Chanel launches the iconic 2.55 quilted handbag. She also releases Pour Monsieur, the only mens fragrance to be produced in Mademoiselle's lifetime.

1957

Mademoiselle Chanel flew to Dallas to recieve the Neiman Marcus Award for Distinguished Service in the Field of Fashion. It was also the year that she opened the New York boutique on 57th Street.

10-1-1970

Gabrielle Chanel introduces No. 19, naming the fragrance after her date of birth.

10-1-1971

Mademoiselle Chanel dies on January 10th.
